A trade-show booth on the OFC floor in LA — fully wrapped in custom hand-painted graffiti, with a second artist turning partner-company logos into graf-style giveaways live, all show long.

Jabil came to OFC (the Optical Fiber Communications Conference) in Los Angeles with a brief most B2B exhibitors don't try: skip the usual product-banner-on-truss approach and let a real graffiti artist handle the booth surfaces. Our writer Jay designed all the artwork wrapping the booth — built around Jabil's tech stack and unmistakably hand-painted in tone. It was the best-looking booth on the floor.

Inside the booth, Jeremy worked the event live: anyone from a partner company who stopped by could ask for their company logo rendered in graf style, watch it get painted, and walk away with the piece. A custom take-home for every conversation — and a much better business card than another USB stick.

This is the format for B2B trade shows where the goal is recall and quality conversations, not click counts. Custom artwork as the booth itself + live painting as the engagement — and a memorable artifact every visitor leaves with.

Eighteen years. Six continents. One idea, done right.

For eighteen years, Graffiti+ has been putting real creative tools in people's hands — in public, at scale, and always with cultural integrity.

Born from the roots of graffiti culture and built for anyone willing to pick up a custom spray can, the platform transforms trade-show floors and corporate booths into shared canvases where artists and visitors paint together in real time. From their home in Vancouver to projects across six continents, Graffiti+ has partnered with Jabil, Johnson & Johnson, Dell, IBM, Nike and Samsung to bring authentic, participatory experiences to audiences everywhere.
