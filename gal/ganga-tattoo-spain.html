A permanent Graffiti+ wall living inside the studio of one of Spain's most internationally recognised tattoo artists.

We installed a permanent interactive LED graffiti wall at Ganga Tattoo Academy in Murcia — the studio of Joaquín Ganga, known globally for hyper-realistic black & grey tattoo work and a clientele that includes Drake, Post Malone and LeBron James. Unlike most Graffiti+ deployments, this one isn't a pop-up or an event — it's a fixture of the academy, used between sessions.

The wall lives there now. Artists in the studio use it to sketch, test compositions, prototype lettering and play with reference. Clients and academy students paint freely between appointments. It's the kind of culture-adjacent space the platform was always meant for: a room where mark-making is already taken seriously and a tool that adds another way to do it.

Different format, same idea — put a real creative tool in a culture's hands and let the room decide what to do with it.

Eighteen years. Six continents. One idea, done right.

For eighteen years, Graffiti+ has been putting real creative tools in people's hands — in public, at scale, and always with cultural integrity.

Born from the roots of graffiti culture and built for anyone willing to pick up a custom spray can, the platform supports both touring brand activations and permanent installations — from the streets of Macau to a tattoo academy in Spain, and from a Samsung concept store in London to a permanent mural at Porsche Centre Richmond. From their home in Vancouver to projects across six continents, Graffiti+ has partnered with artists, retailers and global brands including Nike, Samsung, Louis Vuitton and Chanel to bring authentic, participatory experiences to audiences everywhere.

When the platform earns a permanent spot in a tattoo studio, you know it's doing the right job.
